The Cost of Ignorance

Many companies operate under an unethical calculus: they calculate the cost of notifying the public that they have been hacked—admitting negligence—is higher than the potential fine. So they remain silent. Your private data (medical history, financial credentials, or home address) is traded in dark web markets, and you only find out when the damage is irrevocable.
